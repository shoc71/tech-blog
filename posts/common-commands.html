1. Exploiting Loopholes

There are two ways this can be done. 1. The character has the freedom to explore who they are and what they can do. 2. The character is restricted by rules from doing whatever they want.

There's also the mindset needed for them to be intelligent thinkers or for it have the opposite effect.

1. The character must play by the rules effectively

2. The character mut find the truth hidden within the truth.

The character(s) like the audience first learn of the rules of the games and then actively test and see what they can get away with and what rules are enforced rigorously and what is intentionally left out.

The rules exist to prevent the intelligent character from doing directly what they want so they have to work around the rules to get what they want, through any means necessary (this is where the character trait comes into play.)

The important part is here, is the discovery.

The character must show how they got to the solution and the logic behind it. It's like your math teacher screaming at you to show your work for getting the right solution when you have memorized the answer and wrote it and said "I read the solutions and put it in here."

Traits: - Very Observant - Quick-witted / Improv - Analytical - Deception (a personality trait more than anything) - Confidence (a personality trait more than anything)

2. Remove Directories

Command Prompt (cmd) Linux PowerShell (PS)
Command Prompt > rmdir /s /q .\foldername linux $ rm -fr ~/foldername/filename PowerShell > rm -r -fo .\foldername\filename
The rmdir command parameters used to force the deletion: rm rm
/s Delete the folder with all the sub-folders and files in it. asd asd asd asd
/q Don't prompt for a confirmation.

Assumptions should be treated as assumptions and not like facts waiting to happen. The author shouldn't need to bend over backwards to make THAT one/few character(s) right. educated guesses based on incomplete information, never gets it wrong, and repeating this cycle.
